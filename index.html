Progressive Web Applications: Bridging the Gap

In an increasingly mobile-driven world, users often encounter challenges when using both traditional web applications and native mobile applications. Common troubles include slow loading times, frequent software updates, large storage requirements, and performance constraints across different devices (BigCommerce Team, 2024). These obstacles can lead to user frustration and missed opportunities for businesses. As a solution, Progressive Web Applications (PWAs) have emerged to offer a seamless user experience that blends the best of websites and native apps.

My research explores the current issues faced by both users and developers in adopting traditional web and native mobile apps. We also highlight how PWAs can mitigate these issues. The following findings are based on scholarly research, professional insights, and a small pilot survey conducted to gauge user perspectives on application usage.

Survey Results Overview

I surveyed five anonymous participants about how they primarily access their favorite applications, the challenges they face, factors influencing app or website choice, desired features, and preferred storage limits. All five participants indicated they use a mix of both mobile apps and web browsers, underscoring the demand for flexible, cross-platform access.

The most frequently cited challenges included frequent updates, battery drain issues, and slow loading times. Some participants also mentioned high data usage or performance concerns. These issues reveal why a more universally optimized approach like PWAs could be beneficial.

As shown in the chart, Frequent Updates Required and Battery Drain Issues were each mentioned by three participants, and Slow Loading Times was cited by two. The sentiment that emerged from this pilot survey reflects a general frustration with the overhead of maintaining native apps.
